Notes: conditions

Three forms of command in PsyScript use conditions: if, repeat while, and repeat until.

There are just two forms of condition you can use in these commands: 'is' and 'is in'. The first one checks to see if the two operands match exactly. The second checks to see if the first operand can be found anywhere inside the second operand (a 'substring' check). Here are some examples:

conditionresult
Lis Ltrue
Fis Xfalse
Lis LLLLfalse
CDis ABCDEfalse
conditionresult
Lis in Ltrue
Fis in Xfalse
Lis in LLLLtrue
CDis in ABCDEtrue

Of course, it's pointless testing the above conditions: you already know what the answer is going to be. Instead your condition will probably involve at least one variable. Here are some examples involving variables. In these examples, imagine that the last click was on cell 'C' and that variable X contains 'ABCDE'.

conditionresult
$lastClickis Ctrue
$lastClickis CCCCfalse
Dis $varXfalse
$varXis ABCDEFGHIfalse
$lastClickis $varXfalse
conditionresult
$lastClickis in Ctrue
$lastClickis in CCCCtrue
Dis in $varXtrue
$varXis in ABCDEFGHItrue
$lastClickis in $varXtrue

You can test $lastClick, $lastKey, and any of the variables $varA to $varZ in conditions, testing each one against a fixed piece of text or another variable.

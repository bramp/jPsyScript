Command groups: movie stimulus

This command plays a movie on the display, at the specified position and size. The script doesn't continue until the movie has finished playing, at which point the last frame of the movie disappears from the display and the script continues.

Commands

play movie theFile at (x-pos,y-pos)
theFile -- the file that the movie is stored in
x-pos,y-pos -- position of movie in pixels, relative to the middle of the window
Plays the movie at that place.
Examples:play movie handGrabsCup.mov at (0,0)

Technical notes

There may be a short pause (less than a second) after the movie has finished before the script realises it and carries on.

Allowing the script to change the size of the movie lead to dropped frames while playing, and to a gap at the beginning where no frames were played at all. Worse still, the errors were inconsistent: one subject could see a slightly different thing to another subject. For this reason, the ability to resize movies on the fly was removed.

This command can currently play movies in any of the formats suggested by these extensions:

mov, mpeg, mpg, avi, dv, qt

however you will probably get the best payback from a '.mov' file -- a QuickTime movie. Some movies are encoded using codecs which aren't installed on all computers. These movies may not play at all on some computers, or you may hear sound but see no picture.
